How Fydell works

A simple, modern hiring flow built around real work.

1Send a simulation link

Pick one of four calibrated simulations and send a single secure link.

2Candidate completes real work

A timed, realistic scenario in the browser. Every action is captured.

3Review the evidence report

A clear hiring memo: signals, the work trail, and follow-up questions.

Not another coding test.

Screening tools already tell you whether someone can code or recite a framework. Fydell tests what interviews and take-homes miss, because the simulation is built like the job.

1
An underspecified brief

The goal is clear. The path is not. That gap is where judgment shows up, and where it gets captured.

2
Documents that disagree

Numbers that need checking, and a material risk buried where skimming misses it.

3
A stakeholder who changes the ask

New information lands mid-task. Composure and communication get observed, not claimed.

4
A hard time-box

Twenty to twenty-five minutes forces prioritization. What a candidate chooses to skip is a finding.

5
AI allowed, verification scored

Candidates can use the AI assistant. Whether they check its output before relying on it is part of the evidence.

For candidates

Prove what your resume cannot.

Fydell gives you a chance to show how you actually work through realistic simulations, not keyword filters or pedigree.

The problem

Many candidates can do the job but never get the chance to prove it. Screens reward polished resumes and confident interviews, not real ability.

How Fydell helps

Complete realistic work, show your reasoning, and demonstrate how you communicate. You get a proof summary tied to what you actually did.

What you can prove

Judgment, communication, prioritization, problem solving, adaptability, and how well you verify AI output instead of trusting it blindly.
