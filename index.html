What Fydell measures

Evidence from the work trail - hard skills and professional behavior - not claims on a resume.

Hard-skill evidence

Financial reasoningHow the candidate tests drivers, reconciles cases, and grounds the call in numbers.
Modeling judgmentWhether assumptions are challenged, corrected, and documented instead of accepted at face value.
Risk detectionWhat material risks are named in writing, with enough specificity to act on.
Recommendation qualityWhether the memo is clear, priced, conditioned, and usable by a busy stakeholder.

Behavioral evidence

Communication precisionClarity and usefulness of chat replies and written recommendation language.
AdaptabilityHow the candidate revises work after mid-task updates and new information.
Ownership / follow-throughWhether open loops are closed: model, risks, stakeholder reply, final submit.
Evidence disciplineAssumptions logged, sources opened, and decisions tied to captured actions.
Integrity / professional judgmentWhether the candidate flags conflicts, double-counts, or weak case claims instead of papering over them.
